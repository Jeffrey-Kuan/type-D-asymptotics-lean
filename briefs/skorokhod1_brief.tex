\section*{Setting and definitions (Tier 0)}

Fix $T>0$ (carry $T$ as a parameter; $[0,1]$ as a notation-simplifying default
is acceptable if parameterization fights Mathlib, but prefer general $T$).
\begin{itemize}
\item \underline{C\`adl\`ag}: $f:[0,T]\to\R$ right-continuous on $[0,T)$ with
left limits on $(0,T]$ (Mathlib: \texttt{nhdsWithin}-based; search for existing
c\`adl\`ag/regulated predicates first). Define the type $\D_T$ (subtype or
structure) of c\`adl\`ag functions.
\item \underline{Time changes}: $\Lambda_T$ = strictly increasing continuous
bijections $\lambda:[0,T]\to[0,T]$ with $\lambda(0)=0,\lambda(T)=T$, with the
\underline{log-slope norm}
$\norm\lambda^\circ:=\sup_{0\le s<t\le T}
\big|\log\frac{\lambda(t)-\lambda(s)}{t-s}\big|$ (finite for the relevant
$\lambda$; $\Lambda^\circ_T$ = those with finite norm --- a group under
composition, with $\norm{\lambda^{-1}}^\circ=\norm\lambda^\circ$ and
$\norm{\lambda\circ\mu}^\circ\le\norm\lambda^\circ+\norm\mu^\circ$: prove
these).
\item \underline{Billingsley's metric}:
\[
  d^\circ(f,g):=\inf_{\lambda\in\Lambda^\circ_T}
  \max\big(\norm\lambda^\circ,\ \sup_{t}|f(\lambda(t))-g(t)|\big).
\]
\end{itemize}

\section*{Tier 1: $d^\circ$ is a metric}

Symmetry via $\lambda\mapsto\lambda^{-1}$; triangle via composition (using the
group facts above); $d^\circ(f,g)=0\Rightarrow f=g$: from $d^\circ=0$ extract
$\lambda_n$ with both terms $\to0$; conclude $f(t)=g(t)$ first at continuity
points of $g$ (density of continuity points: the discontinuity set of a
c\`adl\`ag function is countable --- prove this as a standalone lemma, it
recurs), then everywhere by right-continuity. Provide the
\texttt{MetricSpace} instance on $\D_T$ (quotient by nothing --- $d^\circ=0$
implies equality on the nose).

\section*{Tier 2: completeness (Billingsley Thm 12.2 --- the delicate one)}

For a $d^\circ$-Cauchy sequence $(f_n)$: pass to a rapidly Cauchy subsequence
($d^\circ(f_n,f_{n+1})<2^{-n}$ with witnesses $\mu_n$,
$\norm{\mu_n}^\circ<2^{-n}$); form $\lambda_n:=\lim_{m}\mu_{n+m}\circ\cdots
\circ\mu_n$ (uniform convergence of the compositions from the geometric
log-slope bounds --- the key estimate: $\norm{\mu\circ\lambda-\lambda}_\infty$
controlled by $\norm\mu^\circ$; prove carefully), so that
$f_n\circ\lambda_n^{-1}$ is uniformly Cauchy; its uniform limit is c\`adl\`ag
(uniform limits of c\`adl\`ag are c\`adl\`ag --- standalone lemma) and is the
$d^\circ$-limit. Follow Billingsley \S12 closely; his lemmas 12.1--12.3 are
the correct decomposition. This tier is the task's center of gravity ---
budget accordingly.

\section*{Tier 3: separability and Polishness; convergence API}

\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] Separability: functions piecewise-constant on rational partitions
with rational values are $d^\circ$-dense (approximate a c\`adl\`ag $f$ by its
values on a fine partition; the c\`adl\`ag modulus argument in embryonic form
--- keep the partition-approximation lemma reusable, Task 2 needs it).
\item[(b)] The \texttt{PolishSpace} instance (Mathlib class; complete + separable
metric).
\item[(c)] Convergence API for later tasks: $f_n\to f$ in $d^\circ$ iff there
exist $\lambda_n\in\Lambda^\circ_T$ with $\norm{\lambda_n}^\circ\to0$ and
$\sup_t|f_n(\lambda_n(t))-f(t)|\to0$; uniform convergence implies $J_1$;
evaluation $f\mapsto f(t)$ is continuous at every $f$ continuous at $t$;
$f\mapsto\sup_t|f(t)|$ is continuous (or at least Borel --- whichever is
cheap); constants embed isometrically.
\end{itemize}
