\section*{Tier 1: joint (progressive) measurability of the canonical process}

For a measurable $X:\Omega\to\D$, prove joint measurability of the evaluated
process:
\[
  \big([0,1]\times\Omega,\ \mathcal B\otimes\mathcal F\big)\ni(s,\omega)
  \ \longmapsto\ X(\omega)(s)\in\R .
\]
Route (elementary, classical): dyadic right-endpoint approximation
$X^n(s,\omega):=X(\omega)\big(\min(\lceil s2^n\rceil/2^n,1)\big)$ --- each
$X^n$ is jointly measurable (finitely many time-cells; on each, a fixed-time
evaluation, measurable by Task 4's \texttt{measurable\_eval}); and
$X^n(s,\omega)\to X(\omega)(s)$ pointwise since the dyadic approximants
decrease to $s$ FROM THE RIGHT and paths are right-continuous
(\texttt{Skoro.rightContinuous}, proved in Task 5). Conclude the consumer
form: for a measurable $\tau:\Omega\to[0,1]$ (the \texttt{crossTime}
stopping times are measurable),
\[
  (\delta,\omega)\ \longmapsto\ X(\omega)\big(\min(\tau(\omega)+\delta,1)\big)
\]
is jointly measurable on $[0,2\delta_0]\times\Omega$ (composition with the
measurable $(\delta,\omega)\mapsto(\min(\tau(\omega)+\delta,1),\omega)$).
If Mathlib's \texttt{ProgMeasurable} packaging fits cheaply (search for a
right-continuous analogue of
\texttt{StronglyAdapted.progMeasurable\_of\_continuous}; likely absent),
also state it against the coordinate filtration; the bespoke joint-measurability
lemma is what Tier 2 consumes and is the must-have.
